# Safety

This system combines high voltage, liquids, microscopy hardware, pressure-driven
fluidics, and biological samples. Build and operate it only with appropriate
training and local lab approval.

## High Voltage

The actuation module can generate kilovolt-level pulses. Treat every electrode,
wire, connector, and liquid path connected to the module as hazardous.

Minimum precautions:

- use a fully enclosed high-voltage section
- use insulated connectors and strain relief
- add current limiting where possible
- add a clearly marked emergency shutoff
- keep one hand away from the setup during energized measurements
- verify output using a rated high-voltage probe and oscilloscope
- discharge the output before touching the chip or wiring
- never adjust tubing or electrodes while the module is enabled

Do not connect the chip until the high-voltage pulse has been verified on a
dummy load.

## Liquid And Electrical Paths

The chip contains conductive liquid-metal electrodes and liquid samples. Leaks
can create unintended conductive paths.

Before operation:

- inspect the chip for leaks and electrode-channel failures
- keep fluidic tubing mechanically secured
- keep high-voltage wiring physically separated from pump and camera cables
- place absorbent material away from high-voltage terminals
- stop immediately if bubbles, leaks, arcing, or unusual noise appear

## Microscope And Camera

Follow microscope illumination safety rules. Avoid eye exposure to intense light
sources. Secure the camera and printed holders so they cannot fall onto the chip
or stage.

## Biological Samples

Handle cells, media, oil, surfactants, and waste according to local biosafety
and chemical safety procedures.

Recommended practices:

- label all syringes and waste lines
- collect sorted and waste droplets in closed containers
- decontaminate or dispose of chips and tubing after use
- prevent aerosols and spills during disconnection

## Operational Boundaries

This is a research-use system. It is not a clinical device, consumer product,
or turnkey instrument. Every lab must validate electrical safety, sorting
accuracy, and sample compatibility before use.
