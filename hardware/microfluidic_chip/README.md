# Microfluidic Sorting Chip

`droplet_sorting_chip.dwg` is the AutoCAD design file for the sorting chip.

The chip supports:

- upstream droplet generation
- droplet reinjection
- spacing-oil control before the sorting junction
- a three-electrode dielectrophoretic actuation region
- sorted and waste outlets

Key starting geometry:

- main channel width: 145 um
- typical droplet diameter: about 40 um
- typical droplet interval: about 100 um
- electrode width: 20 um
- electrode gap: 20 um
- positive electrode protrusion: 20 um

Recommended fabrication workflow:

1. Fabricate a mold from the channel design.
2. Cast and cure PDMS.
3. Punch inlets and outlets.
4. Bond PDMS to glass.
5. Fill electrode channels with liquid metal.
6. Coat fluidic channels with hydrophobic surface modifier.
7. Inspect the sorting junction and electrode region under the microscope.

Confirm actual channel dimensions after fabrication because shrinkage, mold
height, bonding pressure, and surface treatment can change droplet behavior.
